# CheapStays Host Handbook

**Version 1.0 — Effective upon listing publication**

This handbook sets out what CheapStays expects of every approved host. Read it before you list. It applies to every booking made through the platform.

---

## 1. Your Role and the Platform Boundary

You are an independent host. CheapStays is a booking marketplace — it is not your landlord, property manager, insurer, or emergency responder. CheapStays does not control your property, your guests, your neighbors, or your building management.

As a host you are responsible for:

- The physical condition and legal compliance of your property.
- Every obligation owed to guests who book through CheapStays.
- Complying with the laws, regulations, and rules that apply to your listing.

CheapStays's role is to provide the platform, process payments, and enforce these policies. Where a situation requires action in the real world, that action is yours to take (or the appropriate authority's to take).

---

## 2. Property Authority Requirement

You must have the legal right to list your property before publishing a listing.

Acceptable authority includes:

- **Ownership** — freehold or long-term leasehold title.
- **Lease with subletting permission** — a written lease that expressly permits short-term rental, or written consent from your landlord for this listing.
- **Authorized agent** — a written, signed authorization from the titleholder naming you as their agent for short-term rental purposes.

CheapStays may request evidence of your authority at any time. Listing without authority is grounds for immediate removal and may expose you to legal liability from the actual owner or local authorities.

---

## 3. Insurance

CheapStays does not provide host insurance and is not a co-insurer of your property or your liability to guests.

**Recommendation:** Obtain a short-term rental or home-sharing insurance policy before your first booking. Standard homeowner or renter policies commonly exclude commercial activity, including paying guests. Speak with your insurer to confirm what coverage you have and what gaps exist.

Insurance is your decision. CheapStays will not be liable for losses that would have been covered by a policy you chose not to carry.

---

## 4. Fire and Life Safety Checklist

Every listing must meet the following minimum requirements before its first booking and must be maintained continuously.

| Item | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| Smoke alarms | At least one on each level; tested and functional |
| Carbon monoxide detector | Required wherever gas appliances, oil heating, or attached garage is present |
| Fire extinguisher | One accessible unit in or adjacent to the kitchen |
| Emergency exits | Every bedroom has at least one exit that does not require a key or code from the inside |
| Exit paths | Hallways and exit routes free of obstruction |
| Emergency contact | A phone number guests can call 24/7 for property emergencies |
| Emergency services info | Local emergency numbers (fire, police, ambulance) posted inside the unit |

Failure to maintain any item on this list may result in listing suspension pending correction.

## 5. Building, Condo, and HOA Emergency Plan Disclosure

If your property is within a managed building, condominium, or homeowner association (HOA), you must:

1. **Disclose the building's emergency procedures** to guests at check-in, either in a printed welcome document or a clearly visible posting inside the unit.
2. **Include the building's emergency contact number** (manager, security desk, or on-call line) in your guest information.
3. **Identify evacuation routes and assembly points** specific to your floor or unit.
4. **Not misrepresent** building rules as CheapStays rules, or vice versa.

CheapStays does not have a relationship with your building management and will not mediate building-level disputes. Building rules are your responsibility to follow and to communicate.

---

## 6. Legal Address Requirement

Your listing must display the accurate, complete street address of the property, including unit number where applicable. A false or approximate address is grounds for immediate delisting.

Accurate address information is required for:

- Emergency services response.
- Guest arrival.
- Regulatory compliance in jurisdictions that require short-term rental registration.

If your jurisdiction requires a short-term rental permit, license number, or registration ID, it must be included in your listing as required by local law. CheapStays does not verify permit status but may remove listings where non-compliance is reported and confirmed.

---

## 7. Quiet Hours and Building Rules Disclosure

You must disclose in your listing any rules that limit guest behavior, including:

- **Quiet hours** — start and end time, days of the week.
- **Noise restrictions** — local ordinances or building policies that carry fines or other consequences.
- **Common area rules** — pool hours, gym access, parking restrictions, elevator rules.
- **Pet restrictions** — building or HOA prohibitions that apply regardless of your own preference.
- **Smoking policy** — whether smoking is permitted, and where.
- **Party and event prohibition** — if your building or lease prohibits events, disclose this explicitly.

Undisclosed rules that result in complaints, fines, or eviction proceedings are a host responsibility. CheapStays is not liable for fines imposed by local authorities or building management.

---

## 8. Occupancy and Visitor Limits

Your listing must state a maximum overnight occupancy. This is the maximum number of people who may sleep in the property per night.

- You may not accept bookings that you know will exceed the stated occupancy.
- Guests are responsible for complying with the stated occupancy, and you may decline access or terminate a stay if it is exceeded.
- If your property has a separate visitor policy (daytime guests who do not stay overnight), state it clearly in your listing or house rules.
- Occupancy limits must comply with local fire codes and building rules. Platform limits do not override legal limits.

---

## 9. No Unauthorized Entry

Once a guest has checked in, the property is their private space for the duration of the stay.

You may not enter the property during a booking without the guest's explicit consent, except in a genuine emergency that poses immediate risk to life or property (fire, flood, gas leak, and similar). If you enter without consent in a non-emergency, the guest may terminate the stay with a full refund and you may face account action.

Permitted entry reasons include:

- Pre-arranged maintenance, disclosed before booking and rescheduled with the guest's consent.
- Legal requirement (e.g., a statutory inspection with proper notice).
- Genuine emergency — in which case notify the guest as soon as safely possible.

If a third party (cleaner, property manager, building staff) has access to the property during a booking, disclose this in your listing and inform guests at check-in.

---

## 10. No Hidden or Private-Area Recording

Recording devices are subject to strict rules. Failure to comply is grounds for permanent account termination and may constitute a criminal offense in your jurisdiction.

**Absolutely prohibited:**

- Any recording device — camera, microphone, or sensor — in any bedroom, bathroom, changing area, or other space where guests have a reasonable expectation of privacy.
- Any recording device concealed from guests (hidden cameras, disguised microphones, covert sensors).

**Permitted with prior disclosure:**

- Cameras in exterior areas (front door, driveway, porch, pool deck visible from street) — must be disclosed in your listing before booking and must not capture interior spaces.
- Smart doorbells with video — must be disclosed; must not be directed into the interior.

Disclosure must appear in your listing description, not just in a house manual guests receive after booking.

## 11. Safety, Privacy, and Surveillance Bundle

This section consolidates platform requirements across guest safety, personal privacy, and surveillance in one place for reference.

### 11.1 Digital Safety

- Do not collect guest personal data beyond what is needed to operate the stay.
- Do not share guest data — including contact details, travel patterns, or booking information — with third parties for commercial purposes.
- Smart home devices (locks, thermostats, speakers) must be disclosed in your listing. Any device with a microphone or camera must follow the recording rules in Section 10.
- Wi-Fi networks provided for guest use must be separate from any network you use to manage security cameras or smart devices connected to those cameras.
- Do not attempt to access, intercept, or monitor guest device traffic on any network at the property.

### 11.2 Physical Safety

- The property must be free of hazards that are known to you and not disclosed.
- Swimming pools, hot tubs, balconies, roof access, and similar features with inherent risk must be disclosed. Where local law requires fencing, covers, or safety equipment, these must be in place.
- Weapons stored on the property must be secured in a locked safe and disclosed in the listing. Ammunition must be stored separately.
- Pest infestations known to you must be remediated before a booking, not managed around guests.

## 12. Incident Evidence Process

If an incident occurs at your property during or immediately after a booking — theft, property damage, injury, or a safety concern — follow this process.

1. **Secure the scene** where it is safe to do so. Do not clean, repair, or remove items until documentation is complete (unless required for safety or by authorities).
2. **Photograph or video the scene** before any cleanup. Capture timestamps where possible.
3. **Document the incident in writing** — what happened, when, who was involved, what you observed.
4. **Report through CheapStays** using the incident reporting tool within 48 hours of discovery.
5. **Retain all evidence** — photos, messages, receipts, repair quotes — for a minimum of 12 months.

CheapStays may request evidence in connection with a guest dispute, insurance claim, or regulatory inquiry. Evidence you cannot produce because it was not preserved may limit your options in a dispute.

---

## 13. Crime, Injury, and Surveillance: Authorities First

CheapStays is a technology platform. It is not a law enforcement agency, a court, a licensed investigator, or an emergency service.

**If a crime occurs at your property:** Contact the police. File a report. Obtain the report number.

**If someone is injured at your property:** Contact emergency services. Seek medical attention. Document the incident per Section 12.

**If you believe you have surveillance footage relevant to an incident:** Do not distribute it. Preserve it. Contact the appropriate authority and follow their instructions.

CheapStays will preserve platform records (booking data, messages, transaction records) and cooperate with law enforcement, regulatory agencies, and courts where legally required. CheapStays does not conduct independent investigations, make findings of fault, or act as an arbiter in criminal or civil matters.

Contacting CheapStays does not substitute for contacting the authorities. Both may be necessary; the authorities come first.
