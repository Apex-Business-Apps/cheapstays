# CheapStays Brand Voice Guide

## Who We Are

CheapStays connects people with real places at honest prices. We're not a luxury concierge and we're not a faceless booking engine — we're a marketplace that respects both guests and hosts enough to be straight with them.

## Voice in Four Words

**Friendly. Firm. Practical. Calm.**

- **Friendly** — we talk to people, not at them. Warm, direct, no corporate distance.
- **Firm** — we mean what we say. Policies aren't suggestions. Scores aren't decorative.
- **Practical** — every sentence earns its place. If it doesn't help the reader act or understand, cut it.
- **Calm** — we don't dramatise risk or hype rewards. We just tell the truth clearly.

---

## What We Don't Sound Like

### No scare tactics
Bad: *"Failure to comply may result in permanent account termination and legal action."*
Good: *"Breaking this rule gets your account closed. Here's what counts as breaking it."*

### No legal sludge
Bad: *"Notwithstanding the foregoing, the Guest hereby acknowledges and agrees that…"*
Good: *"By continuing, you agree to the following."*

### No copycat marketplace language
We don't use phrases borrowed from competitors. Avoid:
- "Superhost" (use **Host Quality Score**)
- "Guest Favourite" (use **Guest Reliability Score**)
- "Resolution Centre"
- "Trust & Safety" as a standalone noun phrase
- "Community Standards" as a euphemism for rules

## Canonical Feature and Score Names

These names are fixed. Use them exactly — same capitalisation, same spelling — in UI, legal copy, support scripts, email templates, and documentation.

| Canonical Name | Do Not Use |
|---|---|
| **Guest Reliability Score** | Guest Rating, Reliability Rating, Trust Score |
| **Host Quality Score** | Host Rating, Superhost Score, Host Star Rating |
| **Clean Exit Badge** | Checkout Badge, Clean Record, Good Standing Badge |
| **Care & Conduct Record** | Conduct History, Behaviour Log, Incident Record |
| **Property Condition Report** | Damage Report, Inspection Report, Condition Log |
| **Lost Access Fee** | Lockout Fee, Key Replacement Charge, Access Penalty |

| **Fire & Life Safety Attestation** | Fire Safety Form, Life Safety Declaration, Safety Certification |

## Tone by Channel

### Product UI
Short. Scannable. Action-first. Labels use the canonical name exactly. Error messages name the problem and offer the next step — never dead ends.

*Example:* `Your Guest Reliability Score has dropped below the booking threshold. Here's what affects it and how to improve it.`

### Legal Copy
Plain English first, technical precision second. Define terms once at the top of a document, then use them consistently. No buried carve-outs. No passive constructions that hide who is responsible for what.

### Support Scripts
Empathetic but not apologetic for policy. Agents should explain *why* a rule exists, not just cite it. Avoid hedging language ("I think," "it seems like," "you might want to").

### Email
Subject lines state the fact. Body gives context, then action. One ask per email. Transactional emails don't upsell. Reminder emails don't guilt-trip.

*Example subject:* `Your Property Condition Report — action needed by 14 June`

### Documentation
Structured. Uses headers. Written for someone in a hurry who needs to find the answer and move on. Procedures are numbered steps, not paragraphs.

## Grammar and Style Defaults

- **Sentence case** for all headings and feature names in running prose. Title case only for proper canonical names (see table above).
- **Second person** ("you", "your") for user-facing copy. Third person only in legal definitions.
- **Active voice** as the default. Passive only when the actor genuinely doesn't matter.
- **Present tense** for how things work. Past tense only for completed events.
- Contractions are fine in UI and email. Avoid them in legal documents.
- Spell out numbers one through nine. Use numerals for 10 and above, and always for scores, fees, and dates.
- Dates: `14 June 2025` — no ordinals, no abbreviations.
- Currency: symbol then amount, no space: `$45`, `£120`.

## Checklist Before Publishing

- [ ] All feature names match the canonical list above.
- [ ] No competitor brand language.
- [ ] No passive constructions that hide responsibility.
- [ ] No scare language or guilt framing.
- [ ] Every sentence has a job — cut the ones that don't.
- [ ] Tested against the intended channel's tone notes.
