## Plain-Language Summary

If you stay — or host — for **28 consecutive nights or more**, this Addendum applies on top of the standard Renter Booking Rules and Payment terms. Long stays create extra rights and responsibilities for both parties, including the possibility of tenancy protections under Philippine law. Read this document carefully before confirming a long-stay booking.

---

## 1. When This Addendum Applies

### What this means
This document is automatically incorporated into any booking of 28 or more consecutive nights at the same property.

### Your obligation — both parties
- Acknowledge that a long stay operates differently from a short trip.
- **Hosts:** confirm whether your property is subject to RA 9653 (Rent Control Act) and whether the monthly equivalent of your nightly rate falls within the Act's coverage threshold.
- **Guests:** understand that occupancy beyond 30 consecutive days may, under Philippine law, create rights that neither the platform nor the host can unilaterally override.

### Platform boundary
CheapStays flags bookings of 28+ nights as long stays in the booking flow. The platform cannot determine in real time whether a specific unit is rent-controlled or what tenancy rights apply. Both parties must satisfy themselves of the legal status of the arrangement before confirming.

## 2. Pricing & Payment for Long Stays

### What this means
Long-stay pricing may differ from short-stay nightly rates. The terms must be clear before you book.

### Your obligation — hosts
- State in the listing whether a discounted long-stay rate applies and the minimum commitment that triggers it.
- If payment is to be structured in monthly instalments, describe the billing schedule in the listing notes — verbal agreements are not enforceable through the platform.

### Your obligation — guests
- Pay each instalment on time as stated in the booking confirmation or listing notes.
- Do not rely on informal verbal agreements for rate adjustments or discounts.

### Platform boundary
CheapStays' current payment system charges the full booking total at confirmation. For long stays where monthly billing is preferred, the parties may structure the arrangement as consecutive bookings — however both parties should first obtain legal advice on whether such a structure could be treated as a single tenancy under Philippine law.

## 3. Occupancy Protections Under Philippine Law

### What this means
Once a person has been in continuous occupation for a legally significant period (commonly 30 days or more), removing them may require a formal legal process — regardless of what the booking platform says.

### Your obligation — hosts
- Do not attempt to remove a long-stay guest forcibly or without following the proper legal process.
- If a guest overstays and refuses to vacate, seek qualified legal counsel before taking any action. A barangay conciliation process is typically required before court action.
- Do not cut off utilities, change locks, remove furnishings, or otherwise constructively evict a guest — doing so may expose you to civil or criminal liability under Philippine law.

### Your obligation — guests
- Occupying a property as a long-stay guest does not grant unlimited occupancy rights. Your right to remain is tied to the confirmed booking dates.
- Overstaying beyond your confirmed check-out is a breach of this agreement and may result in a damage claim, account suspension, and a formal removal process.
- If you believe you have acquired statutory tenancy rights, obtain independent legal advice. CheapStays cannot advise you on this.

### Platform boundary
CheapStays is a marketplace intermediary and is not a party to the occupancy agreement. We will support legitimate host requests through platform tools (e.g., flagging an overstay, facilitating communication). We cannot assist with, advise on, or participate in any physical eviction. All occupancy disputes must be resolved through proper Philippine legal channels.

## 4. Notice Requirements

### What this means
Both parties need reasonable advance notice before changes occur — long stays involve real disruption to daily life.

### Your obligation — hosts
- Cancelling a confirmed long-stay booking (other than for verified emergency): give the guest a minimum of **14 days' written notice** via the platform.
- Increasing the rate for a renewed stay: give at least **14 days' notice** before the next billing period begins.

### Your obligation — guests
- Early check-out: give the host at least **7 days' notice** through the platform.
- Extending your stay: submit an extension request through the platform at least **5 days** before your current check-out date.

### Platform boundary
All notices should be sent through CheapStays' platform messaging system to create a time-stamped record. Notices given only by phone call, text message, or verbal exchange outside the platform may be difficult to verify in a dispute.

## 5. Utilities & Property Care

### What this means
Long stays create a shared-living dynamic. Utility responsibilities must be agreed and documented before check-in.

### Your obligation — hosts
- State clearly in the listing which utilities are included in the nightly or monthly rate (e.g., electricity, water, internet).
- If the guest is responsible for any utility bills, specify the billing mechanism in the listing before booking confirmation.
- Maintain the property in habitable condition throughout the stay; carry out necessary repairs within a reasonable time after being notified.

### Your obligation — guests
- Use utilities responsibly. Consumption significantly above normal household use may be charged separately if the listing explicitly states this.
- Report maintenance issues promptly through platform messaging.
- Do not make structural alterations, add fixtures, or paint or modify the property without the host's written consent.

### Platform boundary
CheapStays does not mediate utility-billing disputes. All utility terms must be agreed in the listing before booking. We recommend that hosts clearly itemise utility inclusions and any caps in the listing description.

## 6. Occupants & Subletting

### What this means
Who is in the property matters throughout a long stay.

### Your obligation — guests
- The maximum-guest count stated in the listing applies for the full booking duration.
- Additional visitors staying for more than **3 consecutive nights** should be disclosed to the host.
- Subletting, short-term re-listing, or hosting your own paying guests in a CheapStays property is prohibited.

### Your obligation — hosts
- If your property is in a residential building or subdivision, verify that your HOA or building management rules permit extended third-party occupancy.

### Platform boundary
CheapStays does not monitor who is physically present at a property. Substantiated reports of subletting or occupancy violations will be investigated and may result in listing suspension.

---

## 7. End of Long Stay

### What this means
A smooth check-out after a long stay protects both sides.

### Your obligation — guests
- Return the property in the same condition as received, accounting for ordinary wear.
- Return all keys, access cards, remotes, and security codes.
- Vacate by the agreed check-out time.

### Your obligation — hosts
- Conduct a check-out inspection within **24 hours** of the guest's departure.
- File any damage claim within **48 hours** of check-out (see Payment, Refunds & Incidentals, Section 6).
- If you collected an off-platform security deposit, return it within **7 days** of confirming no damage, unless a valid claim has been submitted.

### Platform boundary
For long stays, CheapStays encourages (but does not require) hosts to conduct a brief mid-stay walkthrough — with the guest's knowledge and consent — to identify maintenance issues early and avoid surprises at check-out.
