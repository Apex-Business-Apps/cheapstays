# Safety Center

This page covers three linked topics: surveillance rules at properties, digital safety on the platform, and how to handle emergencies.

## Surveillance Rules for Hosts

Guests have a right to privacy at your property. Certain spaces are absolutely off-limits for recording devices.

**Banned areas — never place any recording device (camera, microphone, or sensor) in:**
- Bedrooms or sleeping areas
- Bathrooms, toilets, or showers
- Changing areas or dressing rooms
- Any enclosed space where a guest has a reasonable expectation of privacy

**Cameras in common areas (living room, entrance, exterior/driveway) are permitted only if:**
- Disclosed in the listing description **before** the guest books
- Clearly visible — not hidden, embedded, or disguised
- Not pointed in a direction that captures a private area through an adjacent space or window
- Audio recording capability is disabled while guests occupy the property

CheapStays bans all undisclosed surveillance at listed properties. Confirmed illegal surveillance will be referred to Philippine authorities. CheapStays is not liable for surveillance violations by individual hosts.

## What To Do If You Find a Suspicious Device

If you find or suspect a recording device in a private area:

1. Do not tamper with it.
2. Leave the area; document from a safe distance if possible.
3. File a report with CheapStays support immediately at [cheapstays.me@gmail.com](mailto:cheapstays.me@gmail.com).
4. If you believe a crime has occurred, contact local police.

We will investigate and suspend the listing and host account pending findings.

## Protecting Yourself Online

Most scams targeting platform users are social-engineering attacks, not technical breaches.

- Conduct all booking communication through the CheapStays platform.
- Never transfer money outside the platform (e.g., a direct GCash or Maya transfer to a host) as a substitute for or precursor to a platform booking.
- Report any request to pay outside the platform to [cheapstays.me@gmail.com](mailto:cheapstays.me@gmail.com) immediately.

CheapStays will **never** ask you for your sign-in link, a one-time code, or any credential via phone, SMS, or social media. Any such request is a scam.

## Emergencies

In any emergency — fire, medical, crime — **call Philippine emergency services first: 911**. Then notify CheapStays support so we can assist with your booking.

For safety incidents at a property, report them through the support system promptly. Include photos, your booking reference, and the property address.
